Experiment 1: OS Installation

🎯 Aim

To format a PC and successfully install the Windows 10 Operating System.

🛠️ Tools

8GB+ USB Pen Drive, Desktop PC, Rufus Software, Windows 10 ISO File.

⚠️ Safety

Always take a full backup of the C: Drive to prevent data loss before formatting.

📝 Step-by-Step Procedure:

  1. Connect the USB drive to the PC and open the Rufus tool.
  2. Select your downloaded Windows ISO file and click "Start" to make it bootable.
  3. Restart the target PC and continuously press the Boot Menu key (F12, F8, or ESC).
  4. Select the USB drive from the boot menu to launch the installer.
  5. Follow the Windows wizard, format the primary partition, and wait for installation to finish.
✅ Result: Windows 10 is successfully installed and the system is ready to use.

Experiment 1: Staircase Wiring

🎯 Aim

To control a single light bulb from two different locations using 2-way switches.

🛠️ Tools

Two 2-way switches, Bulb, Holder, Connecting wires, Screwdriver, Wire stripper.

⚠️ Safety

Strictly ensure the main MCB/Power supply is turned OFF before touching wires.

📝 Step-by-Step Procedure:

  1. Connect the top terminals of both 2-way switches together using a wire.
  2. Connect the bottom terminals of both 2-way switches together.
  3. Connect the Phase (Live) wire from the main supply to the middle terminal of Switch 1.
  4. Connect the middle terminal of Switch 2 to one terminal of the Bulb holder.
  5. Connect the Neutral wire directly to the second terminal of the Bulb holder.
✅ Result: The bulb can now be independently turned ON or OFF from either switch.

Experiment 1: PCB Soldering

🎯 Aim

To practice perfect soldering of electronic components (Resistors) onto a PCB.

🛠️ Tools

Soldering Iron (25W), Solder Wire (60/40), Flux paste, PCB, Resistors, Wire Cutter.

⚠️ Safety

Never touch the heated iron tip. Wear safety glasses to prevent flux splatter.

📝 Step-by-Step Procedure:

  1. Clean the copper tracks of the PCB and the component legs for better adhesion.
  2. Insert the resistor legs through the holes from the non-copper side.
  3. Apply a tiny amount of flux paste to the target joints.
  4. Touch the hot iron tip to both the pad and the component leg simultaneously for 2 seconds.
  5. Feed solder wire into the joint until it forms a shiny cone, then remove the iron.
  6. Trim the extra wire legs using a micro wire cutter.
✅ Result: Components are electrically and physically secured to the PCB with no dry joints.
